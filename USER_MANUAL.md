## General Navigation

When you log into the MJSIR Tracker, you will be greeted by the main navigation menu which grants access to different sections based on your role:

- **Dashboard:** An overview of your current active items and system notifications.
- **Manuscripts:** The central repository for all submitted manuscripts.
- **My Tasks:** A personalized list of tasks assigned to you.
- **Assignments:** Area for managing and distributing manuscript assignments.
- **EIC Review:** Dedicated area for the EIC to review recommendations and make final decisions.
- **Article Status:** A comprehensive tracker board for active/pending, accepted, and rejected manuscripts.
- **Users:** System user management (restricted to REPO Staff).

---

## User Roles Overview

The system operates using three primary roles, each with specific permissions:

1. **REPO Staff:** Handles intake, pre-screening, initial document uploads, and overall system/user administration.
2. **Editor-in-Chief (EIC):** Oversees the entire process, assigns manuscripts to Associate Editors, and makes the final publication decisions.
3. **Associate Editor (AE):** Manages the peer review process, communicates with reviewers, and provides final recommendations to the EIC.

---

## Guide for REPO Staff

As REPO Staff, you are the gatekeeper of the system. You handle the initial processing of all manuscripts.

### 1. Initial Intake & Prescreening
- Navigate to the **Manuscripts** section.
- Upload new manuscripts received from authors.
- Run external checks (e.g., plagiarism) and upload the **Turnitin reports** alongside the original manuscript files.

### 2. Task Creation & Workflow Initialization
- After uploading the necessary initial files, create a new task for the manuscript.
- Assign the manuscript to the workflow so the Editor-in-Chief (EIC) is notified and can take over the assignment process.

### 3. Managing Final Decisions
- Navigate to the **Article Status** tracker.
- Monitor manuscripts that have received a final decision from the EIC.
- Execute the final administrative steps for "Accepted" or "Rejected" decisions (e.g., archiving, notifying authors).

### 4. User Management
- Navigate to the **Users** section.
- Add new users (REPO Staff or Associate Editors), edit existing user profiles, or remove users from the system securely.

## Guide for Editor-in-Chief (EIC)

As the EIC, you have a high-level view of the entire queue and are responsible for assignments and final decisions.

### 1. Overview & Assignment
- Check the **Dashboard** or **Assignments** section for new manuscripts that have passed prescreening.
- Review the initial documents and Turnitin reports provided by the REPO Staff.
- Provide initial instructions and officially assign the manuscript to an appropriate **Associate Editor (AE)** based on their expertise.

### 2. Reviewing Recommendations
- Navigate to the **EIC Review** section.
- Monitor manuscripts that have completed the peer-review process and have been returned by an AE.
- Read the remarks and recommendations provided by the AE.

### 3. Making Final Decisions
- Based on the AE's recommendation and the peer-review outcomes, make a final decision on the manuscript:
  - **Reject:** The article is not suitable for publication.
  - **Return for Revision:** The author needs to make changes based on feedback.
  - **Accept:** The article is approved for publication.
- The status will be updated in the system, notifying the relevant parties.

## Guide for Associate Editors (AE)

As an Associate Editor, you manage the rigorous peer-review process for assigned manuscripts.

### 1. Receiving Assignments
- Check your **My Tasks** section. You will receive notifications when the EIC assigns a new manuscript to you.
- Review the manuscript details, initial documents, and any specific instructions left by the EIC.

### 2. Managing the Peer-Review Process
- You are responsible for identifying and managing suggested peer reviewers for the manuscript.
- The system supports a **Multi-Round Review Tracking** process (up to 3 rounds).
- For each round:
  - Track revised documents.
  - Provide and track external drive links for review materials.
  - Record the outcomes of the review round.

### 3. Providing Recommendations to EIC
- Once the peer review process is complete (after 1 or more rounds), compile the feedback.
- Upload any reviewed/marked-up documents into the system.
- Provide your final remarks and a concrete recommendation (e.g., Accept, Reject, Major Revision) back to the EIC for their final decision.

## Status Tracking & Management

The MJSIR Tracker provides robust tools for tracking the life cycle of a manuscript:

### Multi-Round Review Tracking
The system handles up to 3 nested rounds of peer review. Associate Editors can update the status of each round independently, ensuring a clear history of revisions and reviewer feedback.

### Action Tracking
Throughout the process, users can utilize dedicated fields to track "Action Taken" documents. This includes storing rejection letters, revision instruction documents, or acceptance certificates, ensuring all correspondence is logged with the manuscript.

### Article Status Dashboard
The **Article Status** view provides a centralized tracking board. It is divided into categories:
- **Active / Pending:** Manuscripts currently moving through the workflow.
- **Accepted:** Manuscripts that have received final approval.
- **Rejected:** Manuscripts that have been declined.

### Real-Time Notifications
The system includes an in-app notification feature. You will be alerted automatically when a manuscript is assigned to you, when its status changes, or when action is required on your part. Always check your notifications for the latest updates on your assigned tasks.
